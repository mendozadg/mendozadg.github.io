About Me

I am a UX generalist working as a UX designer at Indeed.

I completed my Master of Science degree at UT-Austin focusing on user experience design & research. My undergraduate career was in Pre-Columbian archaeology, visual art, and French. So how did I end up in UX? Asides from the fact that I have pursued art, design, and research since middle school, I have always found a way to design better experiences for the primary stakeholders at each job I have held. Whether this was improving the college application and portfolio submission process for students during my time directing the undergraduate admissions for UT-Austin's art and design programs, or creating an exhaustive and cross-referenced manual of best practices for the maintenance of a Salesforce database of museum constituents for my successor to use during her onboarding.

I've worked in nonprofits, museums, and higher education in various capacities. As for my professional experience in UX, that includes:

  • an internship on the internal tools team at Indeed in summer 2018, where I redesigned their onboarding tool for new hires;
  • a graduate assistantship at my school's Healthcare Experience Lab, where I designed a healthcare provider-facing system in collaboration with the Dell Medical School's psychiatry department;
  • a contract consultant role for a Houston-based oil and gas company, where I designed the user experience for their SpeedWise suite of products and allowed them to pivot from consulting services to SaaS
  • and a current role on the Internal Platform UX team at Indeed, where I currently serve as the sole UX member for Product Platform, leading the UX efforts for the account registration and login flows for Indeed.com and its mobile app.
