Indeed

UX design work for Indeed's internal tools UX team

Summary

Role: Associate UX Designer Date: June 2019 - present

Summary

When I first started at Indeed I was embedded within the Data Platform team, focusing on Analytics tools. During this time, I was a part of the following initiatives:

  • I redesigned Indeed's data glossary, Alexandria. Alexandria was riddled with usability issues and a less than optimal search experience. There were three major components of the redesign: the search and filtering experience; the display of data source entries and documentation; and designing a method for users to understand the difference between data quality by the means of certification levels and the reliability of index builds.
  • I proposed and led a design sprint for the IQL team as a way of kicking off the design of a new querying and dashboarding tool named iDash, which consolidated numerous analytics tools into a single place.

After about a year, I was moved to a team that had been in need of UX support for a while - Identity Platform. This team manages the account creation and sign in experience on Indeed, as well as OAuth. Since my start as the sole UXer on this team, I have done the following:

  • Flows and designs for feature explorations and tests, including email-based passwordless login and phone-number-only account;
  • Creating the flow and design for a new OAuth application registration process that allows developers to use Indeed's APIs;
  • Proposing and leading a design workshop that resulted in a north star vision that consolidates various initiatives and allows the team to work towards a more holistic authentication experience;
  • Designing the MVP for single sign-on;
  • Research on two-sided marketplaces and end-to-end UX for designating users as either jobseekers, employers, or both;

While I am unable to show this work publicly on my portfolio, I am happy to discuss in private meetings or interviews. Please reach out for more information.
